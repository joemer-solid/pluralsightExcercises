Points of Consideration:

 

What are the reponsibilities of the class as it is now written?

 

HINT   : When asking this question, think about the class more as a CLASSIFICATION and less about a functional code file. We are concerned initially with

how this classification fits in with the new Virtual Ecosystem we are creating to be modeled after real-world processes. Notice the term below “ORDER KNOWLEDGE”.

 

Classification  is the means whereby we order knowledge . In object-oriented design, recognizing the sameness among things allows us to expose the commonality within key abstractions and mechanisms and eventually leads us to smaller applications and simpler architectures

 

HINT   : Referring to the principle of Separation of Concerns – you can think of the “single concern” of this class – or how it will be modeled within a boundary of other classes

desgined to model this particular separate concern of the application.  Another way to think of this is: “once I have identified this class or classification’s single responsibility, all of the supporting functionality it requires to carry out this responsibility is NOT its concern. Moreover, it will require a design that will enable easy access to this supporting functionality.

These supporting functionalities will be encapsulated and accessed at a high level of abstraction that will absolve this class from ANY RESPONSIBILITY regarding the inner-workings of these supporting functionalities.

 

Separation of Concerns (SoC) – is the process of breaking a computer program into distinct features that overlap in functionality as little as possible. A concern is any piece of interest or focus in a program. Typically, concerns are synonymous with features or behaviors.

 

Single Responsibility Principle (SRP) – every object should have a single responsibility, and that all its services should be narrowly aligned with that responsibility. On some level Cohesion is considered a synonym for SRP.

Footnotes:
Cohesion: the action or fact of forming a united whole.

 

·          Cohesion is used to indicate the degree to which a class has a single, well-focused purpose. Coupling is all about how classes interact with each other, on the other hand cohesion focuses on how single class is designed. Higher the cohesiveness of the class, better is the OO design.

Benefits of Higher Cohesion:

·          Highly cohesive classes are much easier to maintain and less frequently changed.

·          Such classes are more usable than others as they are designed with a well-focused purpose.
